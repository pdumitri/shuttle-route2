P+R Mesenich/Wasserbillig → P+R Héienhaff (Airport) → Eurohub Sud Dudelange
New route optimized for Bus 306 connection from Trier region.

✅ Advantages of P+R Mesenich vs. old Sandweiler-Contern stop:
  • Direct bus connection from Trier via Bus 306 (every 30-60 min, lu-ve)
  • Multiple return options: Bus 306 direct, Bus + Train to Trier Pallien, Train to Trier Hbf

💡 Why This Route Is Better

🎯 Key benefit: Team travels together
With Bus 306 as the feeder, the 5 team members from Trier/Igel area will be on the same bus every morning (course 3521, dep. 07:00 Trier). This means:
  • Single meeting point — everyone boards Bus 306 at their nearest Trier stop and arrives together at P+R Mesenich
  • No coordination needed — the shuttle simply waits for the 07:30 bus arrival
  • Accountability — if one person is on the bus, everyone is
Passengers:
  • 📍 P+R Mesenich — 5 team members (from Trier via Bus 306)
  • 📍 P+R Héienhaff — Gerson (yellow badge) + cleaning team, security, and other yellow badge colleagues who wish to join
🔄 More return flexibility than Sandweiler-Contern:
From P+R Mesenich you have 2 ways home to Trier:
  1. Bus 306 direct — every 30 min in the evening (18:31, 19:01, 19:31...)
  2. Bus + Train — bus to Wasserbillig Gare + train to Trier Pallien or Trier Hbf
Compare with Sandweiler-Contern where you had only 1 option: the CFL train (1-2 per hour).
⚖️ Trade-off (transparency):
The new route is ~17 km longer in total compared to the old one (46 km vs 29 km one-way). The extra distance comes from the new Mesenich→Héienhaff segment (20 km on A1), which replaces the need for individuals to get to Luxembourg by car/train.

However, this is justified because:
  • The extra distance is mostly highway (A1) — fast, predictable, counter-commute direction
  • It eliminates the need for individual car trips to Sandweiler-Contern or Héienhaff
  • The Bus 306 can be covered with the Deutschlandticket for commuting from Germany, reducing the need for anyone to drive to Luxembourg
  • Net time difference is only +19 minutes in the morning compared to old route (48 min vs 29 min from first shuttle stop), mostly on fast highway
  • But the door-to-door time from Trier is actually comparable — old route required getting to Sandweiler by train first (~44 min from Trier Hbf), new route uses Bus 306 (~30 min from Trier to Mesenich)
